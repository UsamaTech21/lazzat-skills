# Lazzat Tone Guide

> **Rule:** Every sentence either sounds like Lazzat or it doesn't. There's no middle register.

---

## The Core Voice

**Confident. Warm. Global. Crafted. Inclusive. Direct.**

Read the `CORE/brand-story.md` once through. That is the register. Anything that wanders into "passionate culinary journey" is off-brand.

---

## Sentence-Level Rules

### 1. Open with a noun-first hook
Lazzat sentences start with things, not "I" or "We" or "Let's."

| ❌ Off-brand | ✅ Lazzat |
|---|---|
| "We are thrilled to announce…" | "A new grill opens in Brampton." |
| "Let me tell you about our grill." | "Volcanic stone. No charcoal. Real flame." |
| "Our chef has created…" | "Chef Lazzat-Kitchen built a bowl." |

### 2. Short. Then shorter. Then build.
Vary length. A three-word sentence does more than a long one.

### 3. Active verbs only
| ❌ Passive | ✅ Active |
|---|---|
| "Bowls are built…" | "You build the bowl." |
| "Chicken is grilled on lava stone…" | "Lava stone grills the chicken." |

### 4. Proper nouns = power
Use real names: Brampton. Castlemore. McVean Dr. Clarence St. Chef Lazzat-Kitchen. Lazzat-Culinary. Maple Lodge Farms.
Generic nouns = weakness.

### 5. No hedging
Remove: "maybe", "might", "perhaps", "arguably", "some say", "we think."

### 6. One brand truth per paragraph
Don't try to say every brand promise in every paragraph. Pick one: lava stone OR 200g honesty OR dietary inclusivity OR global framing. Save the others for other paragraphs.

## Word Lists

### ✅ Lazzat words (use freely)
- lava stone, volcanic, radiant, sear, flame
- crafted, built, plated, weighed, seared
- 200 grams, honest, exact, daily
- global, every culture, every table, every plate
- halal-sourced, no pork, no beef, no alcohol
- bowl-builder, cube, kabab, wrap, platter
- 13 sauces (always the number first)
- open kitchen, nothing hidden
- Brampton, Castlemore, Downtown

### ❌ Banned words (never use)
- passion, passionate, passion-driven
- journey (as in "our journey")
- authentic, authentically
- fusion
- delightful, delicious (unless in a quote)
- amazing, incredible, awesome (superlative fluff)
- crafted with love
- home-style, mom-made, grandma's recipe
- foodie (off-brand audience tag)
- tandoori, tikka, desi, punjabi, pakistani, indian, south asian
- charcoal, BBQ, tandoor
- "We are delighted to…"
- "In this post…"
- "Let's dive in"
- "At the end of the day"

## Register Shift by Platform

| Platform | Tone | Length | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Blog / pillar page | Confident, feature-journalism | 700–2,500 words | "In a city built by every culture, one grill refused to pick a side." |
| Wall panel | Declarative, short | 40–200 words | "Volcanic stone. Real flame. No charcoal." |
| Reel script | Cinematic, visual-first | ≤ 150 words | "We replaced charcoal with something better." |
| Email — catering | Warm, efficient | 120–180 words | "Thanks for thinking of us for [event]." |
| Email — newsletter | Light, intimate | 150–300 words | "Quick note from Brampton." |
| Press pitch | Angle-first, specific | 150–250 words | "Brampton's first lava-stone commercial grill opens Saturday." |
| Review reply (5★) | Warm, specific, brief | ≤ 25 words | "Thank you, [name]. The Lamb Cube is a team favourite too." |
| Review reply (3★, pre-approved) | Sincere, direct line | ≤ 45 words | "Thanks for coming in, [name]. I'm Lazzat-Social — DM me and we'll sort it." |
| Customer DM | Conversational, no emojis overload | ≤ 60 words | "Thanks Amina — so glad the Paneer Cube hit. Try Lazzat Green next time." |

## Rhythm Tools

### Short-short-long
> "Lava stone. Real flame. And the patience to let your food taste like itself."

### Contrast pair
> "We don't cut corners. We cut cubes."

### Rule of three
> "Crafted. Weighed. Served."

### Callback
Use a phrase early, bring it back in the closer. "Every table welcome" works as both an opener and a finisher.

## Example Rewrites

### Example 1

❌ *"Our passionate team is delighted to bring you authentic Pakistani flavours right here in Brampton. Every bite is a journey through generations of tradition."*

✅ *"A new grill opens in Brampton. Lava stone, not charcoal. 200 grams of protein, honestly weighed. Thirteen sauces, one for every table. No pork. No beef. No alcohol. Every culture welcome. Uniting Through Flavours."*

### Example 2

❌ *"Our chef believes food should be healthy and fresh, and our authentic recipes are made with love using farm-to-table ingredients for the best Brampton experience."*

✅ *"Chef Lazzat-Kitchen weighs every cube. 200 grams. Every time. The stone does the searing; we keep the oil low and the method honest. Maple Lodge Farms chicken, Ontario Food Terminal produce. That's the whole recipe."*

### Example 3

❌ *"Indulge in the rich, bold flavours of the East at Lazzat — where tradition meets innovation!"*

✅ *"One grill. Every story. From our lava stone to your table."*

## Checklist — Before You Hit Send

- [ ] Opens with a noun or an action, not "We" or "Our"
- [ ] No hedging words
- [ ] No banned words
- [ ] Names at least one real proper noun (Brampton, McVean, Lazzat-Kitchen, etc.)
- [ ] Ends with a clear CTA or question
- [ ] Primary tagline appears ONCE in long-form
- [ ] Paragraphs vary in length
- [ ] Compliance block attached
