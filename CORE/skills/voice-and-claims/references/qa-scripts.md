# Lazzat - Staff Scripts for Common Customer Questions

All answers are on-brand, regulation-safe, and respect the "no comparison" rule. Staff should be able to answer these verbatim - or close enough - without looking them up. For the why behind each script, see `SKILL.md` in this skill.

---

## Religion, diet, and the menu

### "Is this halal?"
> "Yes, our meat is halal. We just don't lead with it because Lazzat is built for everyone at the table."

*(Staff reminder: this is the ONLY context where the word "halal" is used aloud on the floor. Never volunteer it. Never print it. See `SKILL.md` Section 8.)*

### "Why no beef?"
> "We wanted a menu everyone at the table could share. Beef is off the menu across a lot of our guests' communities - so we built around it."

### "Why no pork?"
> "Same reason we don't serve beef. One menu, no one has to check twice."

### "Is the paneer touching the chicken on the grill?"
> "Never. Every protein has its own lane on the grill, and staff are trained to keep them separate. Vegetarian stays vegetarian - it's a hard rule here."

### "Is this vegan?"
> "Some of our options are vegan. Let me walk you through them - soya skewers, [list salads], no paneer, no yogurt-based sauces."

### "Is this kosher?"
> "We're not kosher-certified, but we don't serve beef, pork, shellfish, or alcohol. If you'd like, I can tell you which items use dairy so you can avoid any combinations you don't want."

## The drinks

### "Why don't you sell Coke?"
> "We only serve real fruit drinks here. No soda, no bubble tea - just blended fresh from real fruit."

### "What's in your shakes?"
> "Real fruit, whole milk, cream, cane sugar, and ice. That's the whole list."

### "Is it a smoothie?"
> "We call them shakes and fruit blends. Everything is blended fresh when you order."

### "Is the fruit fresh?"
> "It's real fruit - some fresh, some frozen, depending on the season. Real whole fruit, not syrups or flavourings."

### "Why is this more expensive than [other chain]?"
> "We use real fruit, real cream, and real cane sugar. That costs more than syrup-based drinks - but it's what we wanted to serve."

*(Never name the other chain back. Never criticize their product.)*

## The food

### "How much meat do I get?"
> "200 grams of protein per plate, minimum. We weigh it before it hits the grill - every order. Same rule for chicken, lamb, paneer, and soya."

### "Is your chicken hormone-free?"
> "Like all Canadian chicken, ours is raised without added hormones - that's been the law in Canada since 1963."

*(Do NOT say "hormone-free" - it's an illegal framing.)*

### "Where does your chicken come from?"
> "Maple Lodge Farms - Canada's largest poultry processor, headquartered right here in Brampton."

### "What makes your sauces different?"
> "Every sauce is developed and made in our own kitchen. No commercial additives, no pre-mixed bases. Seventeen sauces from six continents."

## The brand

### "What does Lazzat mean?"
> "It's a word from our part of the world. Honestly, we'd rather let the food do the explaining."

*(Do NOT define the word linguistically. Keep it implicit.)*

### "Is this a new chain? Are you opening more?"
> "This is our first Lazzat. We've owned and operated twenty-plus restaurants over the last eleven years - this one is what that learning built."

### "Why the salt wall?"
> "Himalayan pink salt. Formed about 600 million years ago from an ancient sea. We thought it was a beautiful thing to build with."

*(NEVER say anything about health, air, minerals as nutrition, or healing.)*

### "Why is the kitchen open?"
> "If you can't watch us cook it, why would you eat it?"

## The founder / team story (if asked)

*(Use college names only after written consent is on file.)*

### "What's different about this place compared to your other restaurants?"

*(NEVER criticize, compare, or rank Osmow's or any of the founders' sister brands. See `SKILL.md` Section 9.)*

### "Are you the Osmow's people?"

*(Confirm the lineage. Don't compare. Don't rank. Move forward.)*

## Complaints (general framework)

### "This [item] isn't right."
> "I'm so sorry - let me make it right. [Replace/refund per authority matrix]. Thank you for telling us."

*(Never argue. Never defend. Fix it.)*

### "I want to talk to a manager."
> "Of course. Let me get them for you right now."

*(No hesitation. Escalate immediately.)*

### "My friend had a bad experience here."
> "I'm sorry to hear that. Would you tell me what happened? I want to make sure we address it - and I want to make sure your visit today goes well."

## Things staff should NEVER say

- "All our competitors use syrups" - comparison.
- "We're better than [X]" - comparison.
- "Our other restaurants weren't as good" / "Osmow's doesn't do this" - disparages own work.
- "We're Pakistani" / "We're Indian" / "We're South Asian" / "We're desi" - breaks globally-neutral positioning.
- "It's healthy" / "It's detox" - regulatory risk.
- "This salt has 84 minerals" / "the salt wall cleans the air" - regulatory risk.
- "Hormone-free chicken" - illegal framing.
- "It's fine, no allergens to worry about" - liability.
- "We're a halal restaurant" as a lead - breaks positioning rule.
