## 2. The 200 Gram Promise (Locked)

**Every protein served at Lazzat is weighed at a minimum of 200 grams before it touches the grill.** Every order. Every time. No eyeballing. A kitchen scale sits on the prep line and the team uses it. Applies to chicken, lamb, paneer, and soya equally.

**Why it matters**
- The customer knows exactly what they are paying for.
- The kitchen cannot short a plate to make the margin work on a bad day.
- It forces the menu price to be honest.
- Easy to enforce. Easy to catch if broken.

**Writing rule:** Always pair the number with the verb "weigh." "200g of chicken" is a spec. "200g, weighed before the grill" is a promise.

**Approved phrasings**
- "200 grams of chicken. Weighed before it hits the grill. Every order."
- "200g of lamb, paneer, or soya. We weigh it. You see it."
- "We put a scale on the prep line because we'd rather be slow than vague."
- "Minimum 200 grams. Every protein. Every plate."
- "The scale doesn't lie."

## 3. The Salt Tile Wall (Locked)

**The ONLY verified claim we ever make about the salt:**

> "Formed approximately 600 million years ago from the evaporation of an ancient sea."

Source: Khan et al. (2026), *Solid Earth Sciences*. This is the defensible figure.

**Optional supporting facts**
- Quarried from the Khewra Salt Mine - world's second-largest salt mine by reserve volume.
- Semi-translucent; wall glows warm amber when backlit.
- Pink colour from naturally occurring iron oxide - no two tiles identical.
- In a climate-controlled restaurant, lasts 15-20+ years.
- Safer wording: "naturally occurring minerals" (NOT "84 essential minerals" or "trace minerals").

**Numbers we NEVER use**

| Number | Why not |
|---|---|
| 800 million years | Refers to broader Salt Range rocks, not the salt deposits. |
| 850 million years | No scientific basis. Marketing inflation. |
| 250 million years | Wrong era (Permian). Khewra salt is older. |

**Never claim:** purifies air, releases negative ions, detoxifies, alkalizing, healing, "reduces EMF," "84 essential minerals," any therapeutic or healing property, healthier than table salt. These create a drug claim under the Food and Drugs Act and are directly refuted by the Salt Therapy Association.

## 4. Why We Removed Syrups, Powders & Additives (Locked)

**We use only:** real fruit, real whole milk, real cream, real cane sugar, ice. That is the complete ingredient list for a Lazzat shake. Five real ingredients. No more.

**The reason, in our own words:**

> "What we can't serve to our own family, we won't serve to yours."

When we tested the alternatives, we found most commercial shake programs are built on flavour syrups and powders with ingredients we could not explain to our own kids at the dinner table. We decided we didn't want to be in that business.

**Approved phrasings**
- "What we can't serve to our own family, we won't serve to yours."
- "What we wouldn't drink ourselves, we won't pour for you."
- "We only make drinks we'd drink ourselves."
- "Real fruit. Real milk. Real cream. Real cane sugar. That's the list."
- "Not syrup. Not flavouring. Real fruit."
- "Blended fresh when you order - never pre-made, never from concentrate."
- "We don't serve soda. We don't serve bubble tea. We serve real fruit drinks."
- "Five ingredients. Everything else is just marketing."

**CRITICAL: Information message, not a comparison**

We state what *we* do. We never name or imply criticism of another chain's drinks. If a customer asks why we are different, the answer is about our choice, not about anyone else's. Approved framing when more depth is needed:

> "We removed syrups, powders, and flavoured mixes from our drink station. Not because others use them - many great brands do - but because we asked ourselves one question: would we serve this to our own family? Would we drink it ourselves before giving it to you? When the answer was 'not quite,' we took it off the line. Real fruit. Real milk. Real cane sugar. That's what's left."

**NEVER say**
- "Unlike other chains..."
- "Competitors use..."
- "Brand X fills their shakes with..."
- Any line that implies another brand's drink is lower quality.

### The 5 real ingredients

| Ingredient | Is | Is NOT |
|---|---|---|
| Real fruit | IQF frozen or fresh whole fruit, blended when you order | Syrup, flavour powder, concentrate, "natural flavouring" |
| Whole milk | 3.25% dairy milk | Milk powder, reconstituted milk, flavoured milk base |
| Cream | Dairy cream | Non-dairy whip, stabilized cream mix |
| Cane sugar | Real cane sugar, measured by the gram | High-fructose corn syrup, artificial sweeteners |
| Ice | Water ice cubes | - |

## 6. Direct-Flame Grills & Protein Separation (Locked)

**Physical separation. No cross-contamination. This is a hard rule - operational and religious, not just marketing.**

- Direct-flame grills - proteins cook over live fire.
- Proteins **never** touch a shared cooking surface.
- Chicken and paneer **never** meet. Same rule for lamb and soya.
- Staff are trained to keep safe distance between meat and vegetarian proteins on the conveyor grill belt.
- Vegetarian proteins stay fully vegetarian. Allergy and dietary control is measurable, not promised.

**Why this is serious**
This is not just operational hygiene. It is a religious commitment: vegetarian customers (including Hindu, Sikh, Jain, and Buddhist customers) must be able to trust that paneer or soya has never contacted meat. Treat this rule the way a kosher kitchen treats dairy-meat separation.

**Approved phrasings**
- "Direct flame. Every protein gets its own lane."
- "Paneer never meets chicken on our grill."
- "Real fire. Real separation. Every order."
- "Live fire. Not a mark. Not a stamp. An actual flame."
- "Vegetarian stays vegetarian. We physically keep it that way."
- "Separate lanes on the conveyor. Trained staff. No shortcuts."
- "Direct flame. Nothing in between."

**Note on "lava stone" terminology:** The existing brand language "lava stone grill" / "volcanic stone" describes the physical cooking medium and remains acceptable for descriptive and legacy copy. For voice-forward writing, prefer "direct flame" / "live fire" framing from the approved-phrasings list above.

Lazzat's menu was built by subtraction, not addition.

> **We wanted to remove the differences that keep people apart - not the similarities that bring them together.**

Beef divides. Pork divides. Bacon divides. Alcohol divides. Soda is syrup in a can, so it went too. Real fruit, flame-grilled protein, warm bread, fresh salad - those are the things every table can share. We kept those. We removed the rest.

### What is NOT on our menu

| Alcohol | Excluded across several communities | "No alcohol." |
| Soft drinks | Artificial syrups + sugar - doesn't belong here | "No soft drinks - real fruit only." |

## 8. Halal - The Handling Rule (Locked)

**Back-of-house: everything is halal.** All meat is halal-certified and halal-sourced. This is non-negotiable operational reality.

**Front-of-house: we never lead with halal.** The word does not appear on signage, menus, social, film, marketing, third-party delivery-platform descriptions, paid marketing, hashtags, SEO titles that Google displays to customers, or any customer-visible surface.

### If a customer asks directly

Do not deflect. Do not deny. Confirm honestly and move on.

**Approved answer (train staff on this exact phrasing):**

> "Yes, our meat is halal. We just don't lead with it because Lazzat is built for everyone at the table."

**Do NOT:**
- Deny or dodge - it damages trust when the customer finds out (Maple Lodge Farms halal sourcing is public).
- Volunteer it to customers who didn't ask.
- Print it on any surface, sticker, window decal, or digital screen.
- Put it in any third-party delivery platform description.
- Include it in any paid marketing, organic social caption, hashtag, or influencer brief.
- Use it in schema.org markup, meta descriptions, alt text, or any HTML that search engines display.

**Supplier naming, not halal framing.** Where a supplier callout is useful (menu card, About page, press) name **Maple Lodge Farms** - Canada's largest poultry processor, headquartered in Brampton. That is the credential. "Halal" is not the credential.

**Why this works:** The positioning rule says Lazzat is for everyone. Halal-branded competitors in Brampton (Peri Peri Shack, Galito's, La Peri Peri) signal "this restaurant is for Muslims." We want to signal "this restaurant is for everyone - and yes, if halal matters to you, we've got you."

## 16. Safe vs Risky Phrasing (Canada)

Four laws govern what Lazzat can say: Food and Drugs Act, Safe Food for Canadians Act, Competition Act, Canadian Code of Advertising Standards. Both literal meaning and general impression are considered. Burden of proof is on us.

| Safe | Risky |
|---|---|
| "Raised without added hormones" (plus required disclaimer) | "Hormone-free" - illegal framing. All Canadian chicken is hormone-free by law since 1963. Creates false uniqueness. |
| "Made fresh on premises" | "Natural" - regulated term, invites a CFIA challenge. |
| "Naturally occurring minerals" | "Rich in 84 essential minerals" - trace minerals, not nutritionally significant. |
| "Real fruit" (defensible - we use real IQF) | "Fresh fruit" - frozen isn't fresh under CFIA. |
| "Contains: Dairy, Gluten" | "Allergen-free" - absolute claim, zero-tolerance enforcement. |
| "Approximately 600 million years old" | "800 million" / "850 million" - not supported for the salt. |
| "Warm amber glow" | "Purifies air / releases negative ions / detoxifies" - reclassifies food as drug. |
| "Lowest real-fruit shake in Brampton" *(with current evidence on file)* | "Better than [Competitor]" - defamation + Competition Act. |

See `references/canadian-ad-law.md` for full regulatory detail.

## 17. Voice Rules

### Sentence rhythm
- Alternate short and long sentences. Two words. Then sixteen with a clause in the middle. Then four.
- One fragment per panel minimum. "Every time." "No exceptions." "That's seven cloves."
- Body copy: 40-55 words. Headlines: 6 words or fewer.
- One unexpected detail per panel - something readers wouldn't guess you'd include.
- End mid-thought, with a fact, or with something unresolved. No neat bows.

### Preferred vocabulary
Fresh. Real. Honest. Straight. Fire. Grill. Weigh. Squeeze. Chop. Specific numbers (200g, 17, 600 million years). Proper nouns (Maple Lodge Farms, Brampton, Khewra). Team roles.

### Tone by panel type

| Panel type | Tone |
|---|---|
| Philosophy (who we are, uniting through flavours) | Warm. Slightly confessional. Team voice ("we"). |
| Process (open kitchen, live fire, fresh drinks) | Direct. Technical-but-plain. Numbers and named suppliers/roles. |

## 18. The Seven Tests - run before any copy goes live

Every piece of copy is disqualified unless it passes all seven.

1. **Specificity.** At least one specific number, proper noun, or named ingredient? "Quality ingredients" fails. "Maple Lodge Farms chicken" passes.
2. **Fragment.** At least one sentence under five words? Break one if not.
3. **"Who wrote this?"** Can you imagine a Lazzat team member saying this out loud in the kitchen without laughing? If it sounds like a website, rewrite.
4. **AI word scan.** Ctrl+F every Tier 1 word in Section 15. Delete on sight.
5. **Structure scan.** Check for "Not just X, but Y," mechanical tricolons, more than one em dash. Break the pattern.
6. **Comparison scan.** Are we criticizing another brand, including our own other restaurants (Osmow's or sister brands)? Rewrite so we speak only about Lazzat.
7. **Wall test.** Read aloud at normal volume from six feet away. Body copy must finish in under 15 seconds; headlines under 3.

### The One Question That Catches Everything

> **"If a journalist asked us to prove this claim tomorrow morning, could we?"**

- Yes → keep.
- No → rewrite.
- Maybe → rewrite.

10. Has the copy passed all seven tests in Section 18? → If not, revise.
