## Framework — How We Read Each Competitor

For every competitor we track:
1. **Position** — how the customer describes them
2. **Menu format** — are they bowl, platter, wrap, mixed?
3. **Price ladder** — entry price point vs premium item
4. **Review velocity** — new GMB reviews / month
5. **Average rating** — current average
6. **Social cadence** — posts / week on IG + TikTok
7. **Photography standard** — pro or amateur
8. **Their gap** — where they are weak
9. **Lazzat's play** — exact counter-move

## Tier A — Direct Competitors (halal grills in Brampton)

### 1. Osmow's (franchise — multi-location)

- **Position:** Convenience-fast-casual Mediterranean / halal wraps
- **Menu format:** Wraps, rice bowls, platters
- **Price ladder:** $12 entry → $25 platter
- **Review velocity:** Strong (franchise pipeline)
- **Rating:** ~4.0–4.3 depending on location
- **Social cadence:** 3–4/week mostly franchise-level (not location-specific)
- **Photography:** Pro, but generic fast-casual
- **Their gap:** No story, no grill differentiator, no dietary inclusivity, overpriced for commodity menu, every location looks identical
- **Our play:**
  - Lead with **lava stone** (they have nothing equivalent)
  - Hero **200g honesty** (they do not weigh publicly)
  - Frame **Brampton-specific** (they are everywhere; we are HERE)
  - Build **signature sauce identity** (they have generic "garlic sauce"; we have 13)

### 2. Paramount Fine Foods

- **Position:** Premium Middle Eastern, franchise-scale
- **Menu format:** Sit-down + takeout; mezze + grills
- **Price ladder:** $18 entry → $40+ platter
- **Review velocity:** Moderate
- **Rating:** ~4.2
- **Social cadence:** 2–3/week, corporate-feeling
- **Photography:** Pro, classical Middle Eastern aesthetic
- **Their gap:** Expensive for takeout market; regionally coded (Middle Eastern, not global); no delivery dominance in Brampton
- **Our play:** Honest pricing + global framing + delivery-first for Brampton

### 3. Local Halal Grills (pool of 6–10 small operators)

- **Position:** Neighbourhood halal, low-price, regional menu
- **Menu format:** Platters + curries + biryani
- **Price ladder:** $8 entry → $18 platter
- **Review velocity:** Low to moderate
- **Rating:** Variable (3.5–4.2)
- **Social cadence:** Inconsistent, often dormant
- **Photography:** Mostly amateur phone photos
- **Their gap:** Inconsistent quality, regional framing, weak digital, no brand story, no format differentiator
- **Our play:**
  - **Production value** on every social post
  - **Global positioning** vs their regional positioning
  - **Build-a-bowl format** they do not offer
  - **Dietary inclusivity** (vegan / vegetarian / Maple Lodge Farms chicken) they ignore

## Tier B — Adjacent-Category Benchmarks (aspirational)

### 4. Chipotle (Canada)

- **Position:** Bowl-builder leader
- **Menu format:** Build-your-bowl — base / protein / salsas / toppings
- **Price:** $13–17
- **Why it matters:** We borrow the **format** and **transparency**, not the cuisine
- **Our play:** Apply the Chipotle format to a Maple Lodge Farms chicken, lava-stone, dietary-inclusive global grill

### 5. Nando's

- **Position:** Flame grill, sauce identity, globally branded
- **Menu format:** Chicken-forward
- **Price:** $15–25
- **Why it matters:** Their sauce ladder (Lemon & Herb → Extra Hot) is brand identity. We can do the same with 17 sauces.
- **Our play:** **Signature sauce identity** — "Lazzat Green / White / Red" become our Nando's moment

### 6. Sweetgreen / Erewhon / Kismet (bowl + wellness brands)

- **Position:** Premium ingredient craft, dietary hero
- **Why it matters:** Photography + dietary language + calm premium tone we pull from
- **Our play:** Match their photography standard; keep their dietary inclusivity without any "clean eating" language

## Insight Summary — Where Lazzat Wins

### The moat (each one is defensible)

1. **Lava stone grill** — zero competitors in Brampton have it
2. **200g honest portions** — nobody publishes a portion promise
3. **13 signature sauces** — identity-level; nobody in halal grill matches
4. **Global framing without "fusion"** — rare positioning in Brampton
5. **Dietary inclusivity by design** — vegan / vegetarian / Maple Lodge Farms chicken on every format
6. **Professor-validated recipes** — credibility signal nobody else has
7. **Open kitchen with character** — humanized founder + chef story
8. **Brampton-rooted, globally-styled** — local trust + bigger brand aesthetic

### Where competitors win (be honest)

- **Brand recognition** — Osmow's / Paramount have years
- **Delivery penetration** — Osmow's is everywhere on Uber Eats
- **Review count** — established players have thousands
- **Price anchoring** — some locals undercut on price

### Counter-moves

| Their strength | Our counter |
|---|---|
| Osmow's ubiquity | Be the one Brampton chooses BECAUSE it is local + specific |
| Paramount polish | Match their production on 10% of the budget using discipline |
| Local halal price | Lead with value, not price: 200g + 17 sauces + 6 salads = more per dollar |
| Sit-down chains | Fast-casual grill + delivery + catering — three revenue modes |
