# Video Recording Guide — Lazzat-Video's Shoot Playbook

> **Rule:** Every shoot follows this. Improvisation on composition is allowed; improvisation on gear spec is not.

---

## Pre-Shoot (24 hours before)

- [ ] Confirm shot list with Lazzat-Social (what's needed this week)
- [ ] Confirm team members on camera (verify uniform + consent)
- [ ] Charge all batteries; bring spares
- [ ] Format memory cards (backup first if existing footage)
- [ ] Confirm audio plan — mic + mic-stand + battery
- [ ] Bring external drive (1 TB minimum) for same-day backup
- [ ] Pack: reference sheet, shot list, lens cloth, gaffer tape
- [ ] Remind team: clean aprons, clean stations, no visible clutter
- [ ] Confirm lighting — if natural light, pick the right hour (see below)

---

## Gear Specification

### Camera
- iPhone 14 Pro or newer (ProRes on external drive) OR
- Sony ZV-1 / FX30 (4K 30fps or 24fps)
- Secondary: iPhone on gimbal for BTS

### Lenses (for mirrorless)
- 24–70mm (standard) — most useful
- 70–200mm (tight dish close-ups)
- 16–35mm (interior wide)

### Audio
- Rode VideoMic Go II on camera (primary)
- Rode Wireless Go II lav on talent (team stories)
- Ambient kitchen sound captured separately

### Lighting
- Aputure MC or Godox ML60 LED — warm key light from 45° left or right
- Bounce card (white foamboard) on opposite side
- NEVER use on-camera flash
- Natural window light preferred for hero dish shots

### Grip
- Tripod (heavy-duty; DJI Tripod for gimbal-compatible)
- Gimbal (DJI Osmo Mobile 6 for phone; DJI RS3 for mirrorless)
- Mini tripod for low-angle kitchen shots

### Storage
- Dual SD cards in camera (mirror mode)
- External SSD (SanDisk 1 TB or Samsung T7) for same-day backup

---

## Setting Expectations with Team

Before shooting people:

- Explain what the clip is for (social reel / ad / brand film)
- Ask explicit consent — record verbal or written (use release form for featured talent)
- Tell them what to say (or NOT to say) — no unapproved claims
- Let them know subtitles will be burned in
- Tell them the video will be reviewed + edited before publish

## Shot List — Every Visit

Minimum 20 usable clips per shoot across these categories. 3 takes minimum per shot.

### Lava stone cooking (high priority)
1. Stone preheat — radiant glow (close-up)
2. Protein placed on stone — first sizzle (macro + slow-mo)
3. Char mark forming (slow-mo)
4. Steam rising from grilled item
5. Chef plating the seared protein

### Dish hero (high priority)
6. Finished bowl — 45° hero shot on dark background
7. Finished platter — overhead flat-lay on cream
8. Sauce drizzle — slow-mo macro
9. Protein-on-protein stack shot
10. Hand reaching in — lifestyle

### Team (medium priority)
11. Team member at station — 3/4 angle
12. Chef Lazzat-Culinary inspecting grill — portrait
13. Wider team shot — 2-3 team members collaborating
14. Lazzat-Culinary / Lazzat-Standards plating on the pass
15. Team member prepping sauces

### Kitchen + BTS (medium priority)
16. Morning setup — lights on, stations prepping
17. Produce delivery — box opening (Ontario Food Terminal)
18. Chicken arrival — Maple Lodge Farms boxes (with logo visible, respectfully)
19. Sauces being portioned into 13 containers

### Storefront (low priority, rotate visits)

22. Signage / logo / awning close-up
23. Door opening, guest entering (consented)

## Lighting by Time of Day

| Time | Light quality | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| 6–8 AM | Soft golden side light | BTS kitchen, produce delivery, exterior |
| 10 AM – 2 PM | Bright daylight, hard shadows | Storefront exterior only (use with shade) |
| 3–5 PM | Warm directional window light | Hero dish shots, lifestyle |
| 6–7 PM | Blue-hour exterior (20 min after sunset) | Storefront at dusk |
| Evening (interior lit) | Warm tungsten + accent | Team stories, guest interior |

Always scout the location's light before committing — kitchen window light changes seasonally.

## Framing Rules

### Dish close-ups
- 45° or 3/4 front-elevated
- Fill frame 60–70% with food
- Shallow depth of field (f/2.8–f/4)
- Focus on the char mark / sear detail
- Keep edges clean — no utensils cutting into frame unless part of the action

### Cooking action
- Below eye level for "action" feel OR tight macro for detail
- Steady on gimbal OR locked on tripod (never handheld for hero)
- Leave headroom / leadroom — food moves across frame, not into edge

### Team portraits
- Eye-level, 3/4 face angle
- Subject slightly off-center (rule of thirds)
- Background: identifiable Lazzat backdrop (grill, open kitchen, brand wall) — blurred
- Subject looks past the camera OR directly at it (not down)

### Vertical vs horizontal
- Film vertical (9:16) for social
- Film horizontal (16:9) for YouTube + brand film archive
- Where possible, capture both orientations per subject — reframe in post

---

## Audio Capture

### For voice (team story, testimonial)
- Lav mic clipped 6–8 inches below subject's chin
- Record a 15-second room tone at start of shoot (silent)
- Quiet kitchen (tell team you're rolling) OR use a dedicated corner
- Monitor headphones during record — check for clipping / handling noise

### For ambient (B-roll kitchen sound)
- Rode VideoMic Go II or Zoom H1 handheld
- Record: stone sizzle, sauce drizzle, plating clink, chopping, door open, guest chatter
- 10 seconds clean of each sound — gives editor flexibility

### Never
- Shoot audio through phone on-camera mic for interviews
- Rely on post-audio replacement — always get on-set clean

## File Management (same day)

1. Dual-card pull: card A + card B formatted and ingested to laptop
2. Backup to external SSD — full copy, not sync
3. Upload to Google Drive folder `Lazzat_Raw/[YYYY-MM-DD]_Shoot`
4. Rename files: `LZT_raw_[YYYY-MM-DD]_[clip#].MP4`
5. Hand off to Lazzat-Brand + Lazzat-Social via shared drive link by end of day
6. Do NOT delete originals until:
   - Edit approved and published
   - Backup confirmed in 2 locations
   - 30 days post-publish passed

## Consent & Release Forms

For any on-camera talent beyond Lazzat employees:

- Simple release form (template held by Lazzat-Brand)
- Written signature or clear verbal consent on camera ("Do I have your permission to film for Lazzat's social media? Yes/No")
- Note their first name + date
- Children (< 18): parent/guardian signature required — no exceptions

Store release forms digitally in Google Drive `Lazzat_Admin/Releases/[YYYY-MM]`.

## Post-Shoot Debrief

Within 48 hours of shoot:

- [ ] Review all 20+ clips with Lazzat-Social
- [ ] Flag top 5 for editing
- [ ] Flag unusable clips (out-of-focus, audio problem) for deletion — AFTER 30 days
- [ ] Tag clips by pillar and format
- [ ] Schedule edit block in calendar

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Shot too tight — no context | Always get a wide establishing shot |
| Flat lighting (front-on flash) | Side light 45° + bounce |
| Shaky handheld | Tripod or gimbal always |
| Forgot ambient sound | 10 sec of each scene's clean ambient |
| Captions forgotten in edit | Burn in during edit, not "maybe later" |
| Talent read a script (felt fake) | Have them answer a question, not read a line |
| Dish shot from above (90°) for hero | 45° or 3/4 for hero; 90° only for flat-lay |
| Stock footage padding | Never — re-shoot instead |
| Music louder than voice | Music ducks to -18 LUFS behind voice |
| Failed to back up same day | Mandatory — no exceptions |
