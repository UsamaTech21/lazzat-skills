## 1. Punjabi Sikh Community

- **Size:** ~163,000 (25.1% of Brampton)
- **Household:** Multi-generational; large family gatherings common on weekends
- **Food habits:**
  - Vegetarian rotation (especially on certain weekdays and during Gurpurab)
  - Strong preference for dairy (lassi-style shakes do well — avoid naming it lassi)
  - Spice tolerance high
  - Many households practice strict no-beef, some no-pork — Lazzat's policy is a perfect match
- **Festivals Lazzat may lean into:**
  - Vaisakhi (Apr 14) — *community moment, family dining peak*
  - Bandi Chhor Divas (often coincides with Diwali, Nov) — distinct from Diwali in framing
  - Gurpurab (Nov)
  - Lohri (Jan 13)
- **Best Lazzat items to feature:**
  - Paneer Cube (vegetarian hero)
  - Veggie Seekh Kabab
  - Any bowl with Cilantro Lime Rice + Greek Salad
  - Mango Madness Shake
  - Pistachio Delight Shake
  - Big Family Platters (for gatherings)
- **Neighbourhood concentration:** Castlemore, Bramalea, Springdale, Heart Lake, parts of Downtown
- **Sensitivity notes:**
  - Do not conflate Punjabi Sikh with "Indian" — many prefer "Punjabi" and the faith identity is distinct
  - Never use "Desi" or "Punjabi cuisine" as descriptors (Lazzat is global, not regional)
  - Beef absence matters — worth referencing "no beef" subtly during Vaisakhi / Gurpurab windows
  - No imagery of religious icons, turbans, or gurdwara symbols in marketing

## 2. Gujarati & South-Asian Hindu Communities

- **Size:** ~3–5% (Gujarati) + broader South-Asian Hindu groups
- **Household:** Multi-generational; extended weekend family meals
- **Food habits:**
  - Large vegetarian share (especially during Navratri's 9-day fast window)
  - Many households practice no-beef year-round; some strict vegetarian only
  - Dairy + pulse-heavy base
- **Festivals:**
  - Diwali (Nov) — biggest cultural moment
  - Navratri (late Sep → Oct) — 9 days of fasting; meat consumption drops
  - Holi (Mar)
  - Dussehra (Oct)
  - Raksha Bandhan (Aug)
- **Best Lazzat items:**
  - Paneer Cube (vegetarian hero)
  - Tofu Cube (vegan hero)
  - Falafel Wrap
  - All 6 Salads (especially Mediterranean + Greek)
  - Mango Magic / Green Glow shakes
  - Rice Pudding dessert
- **Neighbourhood concentration:** Castlemore, Springdale, parts of Bramalea
- **Sensitivity notes:**
  - Navratri is meat-sensitive — pull back meat imagery for those 9 days; hero vegetarian
  - Diwali is wide-appeal — can go bold on community framing (still global tone)
  - Never depict religious deities, lotuses, or diyas on banana-leaves (region-coded)

## 3. Pakistani Muslim Community

- **Size:** ~20,400
- **Household:** Multi-generational; Friday + weekend family dining
- **Food habits:**
  - Halal non-negotiable (Lazzat is Maple Lodge Farms chicken — perfect match)
  - Strong grill + kabab cultural familiarity
  - Ramadan changes consumption rhythm entirely (late-evening Iftar spike)
- **Festivals:**
  - Eid al-Fitr (variable, often Mar/Apr)
  - Eid al-Adha (variable, often Jun/Jul)
  - Ramadan (Feb/Mar) — 30-day evening spike
  - Ashura (awareness; no marketing, solemn observance)
- **Best Lazzat items:**
  - Chicken Seekh Kabab
  - Chapli Kabab *(name the menu item; never call the cuisine)*
  - Lamb Cube
  - Mega Family Platter
  - Chicken Cube Wrap
- **Neighbourhood concentration:** Springdale, Heart Lake, parts of Downtown + Castlemore
- **Sensitivity notes:**
  - **Never say** "Pakistani" or "Desi" in marketing
  - **Never** use JazakAllah / Inshallah / Mashallah in captions
  - **Never** claim "halal-certified" — "Maple Lodge Farms chicken" only
  - Ramadan = deep opportunity, but quiet daytime + energetic evening cadence
  - Ashura: pause celebratory tones for a few days

## 4. Tamil & South-Indian Communities

- **Size:** ~3–4%
- **Household:** Often smaller nuclear + extended; high dining-out frequency
- **Food habits:**
  - Strong vegetarian share
  - Coconut, curry leaves, rice-forward base at home — Lazzat bowls with rice + salads translate
- **Festivals:**
  - Pongal (mid-Jan)
  - Tamil New Year (mid-Apr)
  - Diwali (Nov)
- **Best Lazzat items:**
  - Paneer Cube
  - Tofu Cube
  - All salads
  - Shakes + Fruit Blends
- **Neighbourhood concentration:** Parts of Bramalea, Springdale
- **Sensitivity notes:** Be mindful of Pongal and Tamil New Year — not the same as North-Indian festivals.

## 5. Caribbean & Black Community

- **Size:** ~13.1% (109,000)
- **Household:** Mixed; strong dining-out + delivery habit
- **Food habits:**
  - Bold flavour preference — Peri Peri, Harissa, Chipotle sauces resonate
  - Rice + protein + sauce format is culturally aligned
  - Halal not universal, but "no pork" is often respected
- **Festivals:**
  - Caribbean Carnival / Caribana (Aug — Toronto main, Brampton side)
  - Black History Month (Feb)
  - Diasporic country-specific holidays (Jamaica Independence Aug 6, etc.)
- **Best Lazzat items:**
  - Chicken Cube + Peri Peri / Harissa
  - Lamb Cube + Chipotle
  - Chicken Seekh Kabab Wrap
  - Mango Madness Shake
  - Sweet Chili + Honey Mustard sauce combos
- **Neighbourhood concentration:** Bramalea, Heart Lake, parts of Downtown
- **Sensitivity notes:**
  - Black History Month (Feb) — a genuine community tribute, not a tokenistic post
  - No "jerk chicken" substitutions/positioning (we have menu v14)
  - Photography should actively include Black customers in community features

## Cross-Community Principles

1. **Lazzat never picks a team.** Every profile is a customer segment, not a brand identity.
2. **Every community deserves a featured post at least once per month.**
3. **Sensitivity beats speed.** If a festival is religious or sensitive, delay 24–48h and check with Lazzat-Brand.
4. **Consent for photography always.** Community features use real customers (with written DM consent) or staged with the team (diverse by default).
5. **Avoid regional imagery shortcuts.** Marigolds, turbans, hijabs, diyas, banana leaves — none of these are Lazzat's visual language. Warm cream, charcoal, lava stone, global plates are.
