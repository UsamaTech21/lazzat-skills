# Movement Marketing — Campaigns That Outlive Ads

> **Rule:** A movement is a belief with a format. Lazzat's belief: every table welcome. The format: the four campaigns below.

---

## Campaign 1 — One Table Challenge (ongoing, UGC-driven)

### The idea
Every week, Lazzat features one guest photo of "the most diverse table of the week" — a table of people who wouldn't typically share a restaurant meal.

### The ask
Customers post a photo with the hashtag `#OneTableLazzat` + tag `@lazzatgrill`. Must show 2+ people visible at the table (or a collage). Anyone can nominate their own table OR nominate a friend's.

### The prize
Featured on our feed + stories. One table per week gets a free Mega Platter for their next visit (up to 10 people).

### Why it works
- Makes the customer the hero
- Reinforces "Every Table Welcome" organically
- Creates weekly content without having to commission it
- Naturally drives repeat visits

### Execution
- Print a small card at the table: "Who's at your table tonight?" + hashtag + QR
- Weekly post: "This week's #OneTableLazzat" with screenshots + short note
- Story highlight dedicated to the movement
- Every 4 weeks, compile top tables into a reel ("One Month of Tables")

### Risks
- UGC quality varies → curate carefully; never re-post without consent
- Could be gamed by group-pic spam → set a weekly judging day
- Privacy — never re-post a photo with visible children without parent consent

### Budget
$ ($200/week in free platters)

### Metrics
- `#OneTableLazzat` posts/week
- Re-post engagement vs. baseline
- Repeat visits within 30 days

## Campaign 2 — Real Fire Certificate (ongoing, in-store)

### The idea
Every customer who orders a Mega Platter or Family Platter receives a collectible mini-card: "Certified — Lava-Stone Grilled." Credit-card sized. Charcoal base, gold accent, custom serial number.

### The ask
Collect them. Post them. Trade them with friends.

### Why it works
- Tactile. Takeaway proof. Free advertising.
- Extremely low cost per unit ($0.15/card)
- Naturally shareable (small, photogenic)
- Builds the craft-narrative (lava stone is distinctive)

### Execution
- Commission 5,000 cards per initial run
- Handed out by server / POS staff with every qualifying order
- Optional: back of card has a QR → "Claim your visit on our map" (adds to community UGC map)
- Seasonal variants (gold for anniversary, red for Ramadan respectfully, cream for summer)

### Content play
- Ask customers to post their cards — repost the best
- Cards become collectibles → drive repeat orders

### Budget
$ ($750 for 5,000 cards + variants)

### Metrics
- Cards handed out (POS tracking)
- `#LazzatCertified` (or similar hashtag) post count
- Repeat visit rate of recipients

## Campaign 3 — Feed Brampton (quarterly pulse)

### The idea
For every Mega Platter catering order during specified cultural moments (Ramadan, Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, Diwali, Thanksgiving, Christmas), Lazzat donates a meal to a Brampton food bank or community fridge.

### The ask
Customers know their catering order = a donated meal. No action required.

### Why it works
- Brand-aligned generosity
- Real impact (food banks in Brampton are stretched)
- Publicly logged = transparent
- Ties our catering business to community good

### Execution
- Partner with Knights Table Brampton food bank (established partnership ask)
- Partner with Brampton Community Fridge network
- Log every donated meal publicly on a website page (lazzat.ca/feedbrampton)
- Monthly update post on socials
- Quarterly press pitch to Brampton Guardian + Bramptonist

### Rules
- Never promote a specific religious festival via this campaign alone
- Framing is always community-first, not religion-first
- The donated meal is vegetarian by default (most universal)

### Budget
$ (meal cost only — $10–$15 per donated platter; funded from catering margin)

### Metrics
- Meals donated / quarter
- Press placements
- Catering orders attributed

## Campaign 4 — The Open Kitchen Pledge (weekly)

### The idea
Every Friday 3–3:30 PM, Chef Mudassar does a 30-minute public Q&A in the open kitchen. Any walk-in customer can ask him anything about the lava stone, the menu, the sauces, the craft.

### The ask
Come in. Ask the questions. See the answer cooked.

### Why it works
- Reinforces the "open kitchen" brand promise
- Turns walk-ins into content
- Builds Mudassar's personal brand (Pillar 3 — team stories)
- No ad spend; zero-cost content engine

### Execution
- Announce on website + Instagram bio link
- Live on Instagram for 10 minutes during the session (hook audience online)
- Post recap reel each Saturday: "Yesterday's Open Kitchen" — 5 top questions
- Archive long-form on YouTube as mini-series

### Rules
- Mudassar doesn't give away proprietary recipes (methods, not measurements)
- No political / off-topic questions (he can defer to "you'll have to ask Sir Waqas that one")
- Always filmed; always reviewed for compliance before posting

### Budget
$ ($0 — fits into Mudassar's working hours)

### Metrics
- Live viewers/session
- Post-session reel reach
- Walk-in traffic on Friday afternoons

## Cross-Campaign Integration

These four campaigns reinforce each other:

- **One Table Challenge** featured customers → may receive **Real Fire Certificates**
- **Open Kitchen Pledge** content → cross-published as **social-media-handler** reels
- **Feed Brampton** → integrates with **content-writer** blog posts ("Feed Brampton Q1 Report")
- All four tie back to pillars 2, 3, and 6 (Brampton & Community, Team, Brand Story)

## Retiring a Movement Campaign

If a campaign stops working (engagement drops below baseline for 60 days, or sentiment sours):

1. Soha + Usama review
2. Propose pivot OR sunset
3. Sir Waqas signs off
4. Announce publicly if sunset (honest: "We're replacing X with Y because…")

Movements aren't forever. Keep the ones that still move.
