## Tactic 1 — The Street Flame Unit (Phase 2+)

**Concept:** A portable, wheeled lava stone grill unit that Lazzat rolls out to Brampton public events (with permits). The flame IS the marketing.

**Where to deploy:**
- Gage Park events
- Brampton Farmers' Market (Saturdays downtown, summer)
- Canada Day celebrations
- Brampton Fall Fair
- Cultural festivals (permit-dependent)

**What to do:**
- Grill a signature item live (Chicken Cube or Paneer Cube)
- Free 50g sample cube + sauce
- "Lava-Stone Certified" sticker for selfies
- Selfie-wall with brand-cream backdrop + Arial Black quote

**What NOT to do:**
- Park on private competitor property
- Operate without fire-safety permits
- Give away full platters (becomes a food-distribution issue for Peel Health)

**Budget:** $$$ (unit: $8-12K one-time; events: $500/event)
**Approver:** Lazzat-Founder
**Content yield:** 5+ reels per event day

## Tactic 2 — Hot Sauce Waiver Stunt (Phase 2)

**Concept:** Introduce a limited-edition "13th Sauce" (sku: Lazzat Volcano) so hot, customers must sign a mock "waiver" before ordering. Actors pretend to be on stretchers outside after eating it. Film the whole thing tongue-in-cheek.

**Pre-approval required from Lazzat-Founder — this is marked Phase 2 (month 4+), not launch.**

**What to do:**
- Soft-launch a genuinely hot sauce (habanero base; we can craft it)
- Set up a "Hall of Fame" wall with customer reactions (filmed, consent-given)
- Actors on stretchers outside for 2 launch Saturdays (clearly theatrical)
- Pitch to BlogTO + Narcity + Now Toronto

**Risks + mitigations:**
- Medical-concern optics → always include disclaimers + genuine food-safety protocols
- Could read as mocking illness → keep tone clearly theatrical; actors not looking genuinely ill
- Halal diligence → sauce recipe reviewed by Lazzat-Culinary first

**Budget:** $$ (sauce R&D + actors + filming)
**Approver:** Lazzat-Founder
**Content yield:** 10+ reels from the reaction wall alone

## Tactic 3 — Free Platter Drop (Phase 1)

**Concept:** Unannounced delivery of a Mega Platter to a Brampton civic group — fire station, mail sorting depot, hospital ward, city hall staff room. Random. Generous. Filmed.

**Who to drop to (rotating list):**
- Brampton Fire Stations (all 15 in the city rotating)
- Canada Post Brampton sorting depot
- Peel Regional Police HQ staff room
- Brampton City Hall
- Bramalea Civic Hospital cafeteria staff (ask their management first)
- Peel Memorial Centre — night staff
- Brampton Transit bus depot

**What to do:**
- Show up unannounced with platter + note
- Note card: "Thanks for keeping Brampton moving. From Lazzat Grill & Shakes."
- Ask permission to film the drop (sometimes yes, sometimes no — respect answer)
- NEVER post faces of staff without written consent
- Filmed from angles that center the platter + location name

**What NOT to do:**
- Drop to for-profit businesses
- Drop during emergencies
- Ask the recipient to post

**Budget:** $ ($120–$200 per platter)
**Approver:** Lazzat-Brand (routine) / Lazzat-Founder (first-of-kind)
**Content yield:** 1 reel + 1 story + 1 GMB post per drop

## Tactic 5 — Ethical Competitor Ambush (Phase 2)

**Concept:** Rather than ambush a competitor's property, we set up our outdoor Street Flame Unit in front of our own store during the competitor's known peak hour. Customers walking nearby see a grill in action — which no competitor offers.

**What to do:**
- Street Flame Unit on Lazzat property curbside (check City bylaw for commercial curbside display — may need permit)
- Hand out free sauce samples (not full meals)
- Selfie moment with Chef Lazzat-Kitchen and the grill
- Film for reel montage

**What NOT to do:**
- Stand on competitor's property
- Distribute anti-competitor flyers
- Name or reference the competitor anywhere
- Hand out discount codes targeting competitor's menu

**Budget:** $
**Approver:** Lazzat-Founder
**Content yield:** 3 reels per session

## Tactic 6 — In-Store Photo Wall (Phase 1)

**Concept:** Build a single wall inside each location as a designated photo-wall. Warm cream base, oversized "One grill. Every story." in Arial Black, gold-rule line, and a subtle wordmark bottom-right. Every customer photo becomes a free ad.

**What to do:**
- Design brief sent to graphic-designer
- Install before launch
- Staff cue: "If you get a photo, tag @lazzatgrill and we'll re-share"
- Seasonal overlays (Diwali LED flowers, Canada Day maple leaves) — swap quarterly

**What NOT to do:**
- Brand-heavy "Follow us on IG" graffiti
- Crowded logo-walls
- Requiring a tag for a free drink (reads as forced)

**Budget:** $ ($800 one-time install + $100/quarter swap)
**Approver:** Lazzat-Social → Lazzat-Brand
**Content yield:** Dozens of UGC posts weekly (ongoing)

## Tactic 8 — "We Buried the Charcoal" (Earth Day stunt)

**Concept:** On Earth Day (April 22), Lazzat literally buries a bag of charcoal in a permit-approved public plot. Plants a tree over it. Films. Pitches.

**Symbolic message:** Lazzat replaces charcoal with lava stone — so we're burying the past method.

**What to do:**
- Get permit from City of Brampton Parks department for designated community garden
- Plant a real tree (sponsor the tree fee via local nursery)
- Film: founder (Lazzat-Founder) with shovel + charcoal bag + tree; 2-min documentary cut
- Press pitch to BlogTO + Narcity + Global News Brampton bureau + Toronto Star food desk
- Tie into Earth Day organic social push (no paid spend)

**Risks + mitigations:**
- Reads as performative → counter by sponsoring real Brampton tree-planting ($500 to Forests Ontario)
- Fire/safety concern → no combustion; just literal burial
- Competitor accusations → zero competitor naming

**Budget:** $$ ($500 tree sponsorship + filming + permits)
**Approver:** Lazzat-Founder
**Content yield:** 1 documentary cut + 5 short reels + 3 press placements

## Tactic 9 — Lazzat Langar (Vaisakhi 2027)

**Concept:** On Vaisakhi morning (April 13, 2027), Lazzat sets up at McVean Dr and offers 50 free vegetarian grilled-veg platters to walk-ins, respectful of the langar tradition of community free food.

**Why this works for Lazzat:**
- Brampton's large Sikh community — Vaisakhi is central
- Vegetarian-only offering respects both langar norms and halal certification pending status
- Open kitchen visible; lava stone visible
- Framing: community gift, not marketing stunt

**What to do:**
- Vegetarian Paneer + Grilled Veg Platter, 50 portions
- Free, first-come-first-served
- Staff on hand to explain (not push)
- Film from respectful distance; only post with consent from any identifiable guest

**What NOT to do:**
- Call it a "langar" in our own marketing — respectful acknowledgment, not appropriation
- Serve non-vegetarian that day
- Religious iconography in marketing
- Gurdwara partnership unless explicitly invited and approved

**Budget:** $ ($300–$500 in ingredients)
**Approver:** Lazzat-Founder (mandatory — cultural sensitivity)
**Content yield:** 1 post + 1 reel only (low-key intentionally)

## Every Stunt Must Ship With:

1. **Why now:** what cultural / calendar / brand moment justifies it
2. **Community gift:** what does Brampton get from this
3. **Content plan:** what we'll film, cut, post (pre-, during-, post-)
4. **Budget + logistics:** dollars, permits, team, suppliers
5. **Risk + mitigation:** what could go wrong, how we've planned for it
6. **Compliance Block:** brand-compliance-checker pass
7. **Approver chain:** Lazzat-Brand → Lazzat-Founder
