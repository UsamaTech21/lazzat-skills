## 1. BRAND ESSENCE

**Name:** Lazzat Grill & Shakes
**Meaning:** *Lazzat* — a word from the founders' part of the world, intentionally multicultural. **We do not define its linguistic origin publicly.** Never positioned as "Urdu word," "Pakistani origin," or attached to any single language or region. Let the food do the explaining.

**One-Line Positioning:**
> A globally-neutral fast-casual grill-and-shakes brand, where every table shares one menu.

*(Do not use "premium" in customer-facing copy — Tier 1 corporate filler. See voice-and-claims Section 15.)*

**Primary Tagline:** *Grill & Shakes — Uniting Through Flavours*
**Secondary Tagline:** *Grill & Shakes — Flavours From Around The World*

Use primary on brand film, in-store walls, long-form, hero panels, press.
Use secondary on delivery platforms, website headers, short-form social, packaging callouts.

**Category framing:** Fast-casual, globally-neutral grill-and-shakes brand. **Never** marketed as South Asian, Indian, desi, Pakistani, or ethnic.

**Founding team shorthand (for press / About pages):** Eleven founders. Five countries. Four faiths. 20+ restaurants over 11+ years of operating experience.

### Supporting Palette
| Role | Name | HEX | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|

| Neutral | Off-White | `#FAFAF7` | Body backgrounds, websites |

### Rules
- **70/20/10 split:** 70% Charcoal + Cream neutrals, 20% Ember Amber accents, 10% everything else.

- Never use Lazzat Red as a primary background. It is a legacy accent only.
- Gold (`#D4AC0D`) from v1 is **deprecated** — replaced by Ember Amber.

## 4. TYPOGRAPHY

### Primary
- **Headlines:** Arial Black or Montserrat Black (weight 900)
- **Subheads:** Arial Bold or Montserrat Bold (weight 700)
- **Body:** Arial Regular or Calibri Regular (weight 400)
- **Accent / Display:** Playfair Display (serif — for brand film lower-thirds and premium print only)

### Rules
- Never mix more than 2 type families in one piece.
- Headlines ALL-CAPS or Sentence Case (never Title Case).
- Body never ALL-CAPS.
- Line-height minimum 1.4× font size for readability.
- Never use script fonts, handwritten fonts, or "ethnic" decorative fonts.

---

## 5. LOGO USAGE

- Primary lockup: wordmark "Lazzat" + tagline under
- Monogram: "L" with lava-stone dot — used only as avatar / favicon
- Clear space: minimum of 1× "L" height on all sides
- Minimum size: 32px digital, 20mm print
- **NEVER:** rotate, distort, add gradients, recolor, outline, add drop-shadow, place on busy imagery without a solid base

## 6. PHOTOGRAPHY STYLE

### Shot List Rules
- **Overhead flat-lays** on warm cream surfaces (wooden, stone, or linen)
- **Hero shots** against charcoal or lava stone texture backgrounds
- **Action shots** of lava stone grilling — real flame, real smoke, no CGI
- **People shots** — diverse, multi-generational, multi-ethnic ALWAYS in frame when people are shown

### Lighting
- Warm natural light or golden hour
- No cold blue tones, no neon, no clinical white
- Slight grain acceptable; plastic-smooth retouching forbidden

### Props
- Warm cream linen, aged wood, brass/copper accents, lava stone shards
- NEVER: red-checkered tablecloths, banana leaves, marigold flowers, mirror-work textiles (all region-coded)

## 7. BRAND VOICE & LANGUAGE

> **Authoritative source:** `CORE/skills/voice-and-claims/SKILL.md` Sections 14–20. Read that file for the full AI-word ban list, the seven tests, and all approved phrasings. This section is a quick-reference; any conflict resolves to the voice-and-claims skill.

### Always Say
- **Direct flame / live fire / lava stone** — the three approved ways to describe the grill. Prefer "direct flame" for voice-forward copy.
- **Real fruit** (not "fresh fruit" when it's frozen)
- **Shake / Fruit Blend** (not "smoothie" or "juice")
- **Maple Lodge Farms chicken** — the named-supplier credential (replaces any "halal-sourced" customer-facing framing)
- **200 grams, weighed before the grill** — all four proteins equally
- **17 sauces from six continents**
- **Approximately 600 million years** — the only salt age we ever use
- **Cube / Kabab / Wrap / Platter** — our four menu formats
- **Chicken Tikka / Lamb Seekh / Paneer / Soya** — the four approved protein-skewer names

### Never Say
- **AI Tier 1 shibboleths** — delve, tapestry, realm, landscape, leverage, robust, seamless, myriad, plethora, testament to, navigate, foster, harness, unlock, paradigm, cutting-edge, revolutionize, showcasing, pivotal, meticulous, vibrant, unparalleled, underscore, synergy, groundbreaking, transformative, holistic, empower, streamline, unprecedented, multifaceted. *(Full list in voice-and-claims Section 15.)*
- **Corporate filler** — premium, elevated, curated, artisanal, bespoke, finest ingredients, carefully selected, committed to, passionate about, dedicated to excellence, our talented team, our valued guests, seamless, flawless.
- **Filler phrases** — furthermore, moreover, notably, "it's important to note," "in today's ever-evolving world," "in summary," "at its core," "a rich tapestry of."
- **Halal** on any customer-facing surface — signage, menus, social, film, ads, hashtags, delivery-platform descriptions, SEO meta, schema.org, alt text. *(Confirm honestly in person if asked — see voice-and-claims Section 8 for the one approved line.)*
- **Smoothie / Juice (as category) / Fresh fruit (if frozen) / Organic (uncertified) / Hormone-free** — all regulatorily risky.
- **Region coding** — Pakistani, Desi, Punjabi, Indian, South Asian, ethnic. **Exception for menu item names only:** "Chicken Tikka" and "Lamb Seekh" are approved skewer names. The ban on dish names like "Tandoori Chicken / Tikka Masala / Biryani / Karahi / Haleem / Nihari / Butter Chicken" still stands.
- **Religious phrases** in marketing copy (JazakAllah / Inshallah / Mashallah).
- **Superlatives** — "Best in Brampton" / "#1" / "voted best" / "cheapest shake in town" / "unbeatable value."
- **Health / therapeutic claims** — immunity, detox, superfood, healing, alkalizing, purifying, negative ions, "84 essential minerals," "healthier than table salt."
- **Competitor names** in customer copy — Cava, Chipotle, Sweetgreen, Nando's, Booster Juice, McDonald's, Tim Hortons, Peri Peri Shack, Galito's, La Peri Peri, El's Lapte, Holy Shakes, or any other brand. *(Internal benchmarking in `senior-strategist` is fine.)*
- **Disparagement of Osmow's or sister brands** — the founders' existing restaurants. Forward-looking / "twenty restaurants taught us" framing only. *(See voice-and-claims Section 9 — absolute rule, no exceptions.)*

### Sentence Patterns (templates)
- "200 grams. Weighed before the grill. Every order."
- "No beef. No pork. No bacon. No alcohol. No soft drinks."
- "Real fruit. Real milk. Real cream. Real cane sugar. That's the list."
- "A table where everyone belongs."
- "Direct flame. Nothing in between."

### The seven tests (run on every piece of copy)

1. Specificity — at least one number, proper noun, or named ingredient.
2. Fragment — at least one sentence under five words.
3. "Who wrote this?" — would a team member say this in the kitchen?
4. AI word scan — Ctrl+F the Tier 1 list. Delete on sight.
5. Structure scan — no "Not just X, but Y," no mechanical tricolons, no more than one em dash.
6. Comparison scan — are we naming or implying anything about another brand (including Osmow's)?
7. Wall test — read aloud from six feet. Body copy under 15 seconds; headlines under 3.

*(Full detail: `CORE/skills/voice-and-claims/SKILL.md` Section 18.)*

## 9. BRAND PROMISE (FIVE)

1. **200 grams of every protein. Weighed before the grill. Every order.** (Chicken, lamb, paneer, soya — same rule.)
2. **Direct flame. Physical protein separation. Paneer never meets chicken.**
3. **Real fruit. Real milk. Real cream. Real cane sugar. Real ice. Five ingredients. No syrups, no powders, no concentrates.**
4. **A table where everyone belongs. No beef. No pork. No bacon. No shellfish. No alcohol. No soft drinks.**
5. **Open kitchen. Nothing hidden.**
