## FRONT-END TEAM — THE 11 FILM PACKAGE FOUNDERS

Eleven people from five countries and four faiths. Each member has a role, a place in the restaurant, a wardrobe note, and an emotional register. The film casts real people into real positions — no acting. These bios mirror the Film Package character sheet exactly.

---

## 1. WAQAS MUKHTAR — Founder & CEO · `Lazzat-Founder`

- **Full legal name:** Waqas Mukhtar. Public brand-copy honorific: "Sir Waqas." Legal / governance / LICENSE / marketplace author metadata: "Waqas Mukhtar."
- **Who:** Pakistani Muslim, 39.
- **Role:** Brand vision, capital, culture, final approval on everything public.
- **Where to shoot:** Front of house, at the pass where the open kitchen meets the dining room. Also in an empty restaurant space for the reflective interview beats.
- **Wardrobe:** Crisp navy or black button-down, sleeves rolled. No jacket. Watch with a worn band. No logo on his chest — he doesn't need one.
- **On-camera:** Calm authority. Dry humor. Softens when he talks about his family. The narrative spine of the whole film.
- **Emotional register:** Controlled on the surface, deeply invested underneath. Gets quieter when the subject matters more, not louder.
- **Story angle:** First-generation builder. 20+ restaurants of operational experience. Wants Lazzat to be the grill brand every culture in Brampton feels ownership of.
- **Never:** Publish quotes from him without his review. Never translate his words.

## 2. MUDASSAR MUKHTAR — Co-Founder, Construction & Planning · `Lazzat-Build`

- **Full legal name:** Mudassar Mukhtar. Public brand-copy form: "Mudassar." Legal / governance metadata: "Mudassar Mukhtar."
- **Who:** Pakistani Muslim, 41. *(Internal only: brother of Waqas — see §0.)*
- **Role (public):** Co-Founder, Construction & Planning. Owns the physical build: renovations, site expansion, second-location buildout. Salt wall install.
- **Role (internal):** Co-equal voice to Sir Waqas on anything physical-space-related. Veto on construction/build decisions. *(Internal-only relationship note in Section 0.)*
- **Where to shoot:** Walking the space with blueprints in his hand. Pointing at where walls used to be. Standing in front of the salt wall he helped install.
- **Wardrobe:** Work shirt, darker tones. Practical. A tape measure clipped somewhere visible.
- **On-camera:** Older brother energy. Protective, methodical, the person who makes sure the idea actually gets built. Prouder than he lets on.
- **Emotional register:** Understated. His love for his brother's vision comes through in how carefully he describes the build — not in what he says about it.
- **Story angle:** The craftsman co-founder. "My brother told me he wanted a room where nobody had to check. I didn't ask more questions — I just started drawing."
- **Signature moment:** The Himalayan salt wall. Every block placed by hand. "When the lighting went in behind it and we flipped the switch for the first time — the whole room changed."
- **Never:** Frame him publicly as "Head Chef" or anything kitchen-related — he is Construction & Planning. Never as "junior" to Sir Waqas or "employee" — he is the co-founder.

## 3. REMON FARAG — Food Safety & Standards · `Lazzat-Standards`

- **Who:** Egyptian Christian, 45.
- **Role:** Food safety lead. Deliveries, temperatures, allergen protocol, halal-sourcing verification, cleanliness audits. Every cooler, every warmer, every receiving door — his domain.
- **Where to shoot:** At the prep counter, at the walk-in, with a clipboard at the receiving door. The person who checks every delivery.
- **Wardrobe:** White chef's jacket, cleaner than anyone else's in the building. Pen clipped to the chest.
- **On-camera:** Precise, unhurried, serious in the best sense. The person nobody argues with because nobody wants to be the one who's wrong.
- **Emotional register:** Quiet pride. He's worked in enough kitchens to know this one is built differently.
- **Story angle:** An Egyptian Christian overseeing food safety for a restaurant built around religious inclusivity. *"Everybody's standard is the standard. I don't have to ask whose rules I'm following. I'm following all of them."*
- **Signature moment:** First-thing-in-the-morning temperature check. "If a number is wrong, nothing starts until it's right. That's not optional."
- **Never:** Frame him as a chef or grill person — he is Food Safety & Standards. His authority is regulatory, not culinary.

## 4. SOHA AHMED — Operations & Procedures · `Lazzat-Operations`

- **Who:** UK-raised Pakistani Muslim, 25. *(Internal only: Sir Waqas's niece — see §0.)*
- **Role (public):** Operations & Procedures. Owns SOPs, systems, rush-hour coordination, cross-station protocols, staff procedures. British-inflected English is a texture the film uses — another voice in the global chorus.
- **Role (internal):** Senior to the brand team by family standing. Final approver on routine brand & social content (Lazzat-Brand escalates to her before Lazzat-Founder). Can escalate directly to Lazzat-Founder at any time. *(Internal-only relationship note in Section 0.)*
- **Where to shoot:** Moving between stations with a tablet. In the back office with SOPs on a whiteboard. On the floor during a rush, calm in the chaos.
- **Wardrobe:** Modern, professional — structured blazer or a clean long-sleeve top. Hijab if she wears one (entirely her call). Practical shoes.
- **On-camera:** Young, sharp, already carries herself like a veteran. Quietly confident. The generational bridge.
- **Emotional register:** Don't push her for big emotions. Let her talk about systems and the character comes through.
- **Story angle:** "I've worked in kitchens in London where every other table was a negotiation. When Sir Waqas described Lazzat, I recognized the problem he was solving. I wanted in."
- **Signature concept:** "A checklist for respect. Every station has one. Who touches what, what gets cooked where, what never goes near what. It sounds like rules. It's actually a promise."
- **Never:** Frame her publicly as "Social & Community Lead" or "junior" — she is Operations & Procedures, a senior approver, and a primary voice on internal systems.

## 5. PROFESSOR PARVEEN — Culinary Advisor / Professor · `Lazzat-Culinary`

- **Who:** Indian Hindu, 50+.
- **Role:** Culinary Advisor. Independent advisor. Validates every recipe and every seasonal drop. 35 years in kitchens. Currently teaches at **George Brown** and **Sheridan**.
- **Where to shoot:** Three locations — a George Brown classroom demonstrating to students, a Sheridan lab, and the Lazzat kitchen walking the line. The visual rhyme of the film lives in him.
- **Wardrobe:** Pressed white chef's jacket at Lazzat. Blazer and button-down when teaching. Reading glasses tucked in a chest pocket. Silver hair, cleanly cut.
- **On-camera:** Quiet authority. Doesn't fill silence. Tastes with a small spoon and closes his eyes for one second when something matters. Nods once, not twice.
- **Emotional register:** Calm, warm once earned, absolute. The emotional beat: when he tastes the finished menu and says *"Yes. This is ready."* Hold that shot.
- **Story angle:** The question Lazzat is asking is the question he asks his students — *how do you feed everybody without compromising craft?* Most restaurants pick one. Lazzat tries both. He wanted to see if it was possible.
- **Publishing rule:** Written consent required before naming George Brown or Sheridan in any commercial / paid output (faculty policies may apply).
- **Never:** Claim he is a co-founder or investor — he is an independent advisor. Never overstate his scope: his authority is recipes + menu + culinary sign-off, not operations.

## 11. MEHUL — Grill *(front-line)*

- **Who:** Indian Hindu, 20+.
- **Role:** Live-fire grill. Flames behind him, tongs in hand. The film's most cinematic character. The grill master archetype made real.
- **Where to shoot:** At the grill. Flames. 120fps footage.
- **Wardrobe:** Heat-tolerant apron, sleeves rolled high. Small burn marks on the forearms — don't hide them, they're the résumé.
- **On-camera:** Young, focused, in the zone. Reads fire by sound. Let the camera spend real time just watching him work before he says a word.
- **Emotional register:** Concentrated intensity that cracks into a small smile when a cook comes off right. The advisor's (Prof. Parveen's) approval means everything to him.
- **Signature line:** "I don't cook fast. I cook right. You listen for the sound — the fat changes pitch when it's ready. That's when you flip."
- **Signature moment:** Prof. Parveen standing next to him at the station — the silent "one extra breath" before the nod.
- **Never:** Replace him as "the grill person" — he is the grill master. Any content about the grill station hero-shots him.
